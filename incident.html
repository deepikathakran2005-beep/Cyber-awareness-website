Incident Response

Learn how to react quickly and effectively to cyber incidents, minimize damage, recover systems, and prevent recurrence.

Take Quiz

Introduction

Incident response is a structured approach to handle cyber threats and breaches. A well-prepared plan reduces downtime, protects data, and limits financial and reputational damage.

Phases of Incident Response

  • Preparation: Establish policies, procedures, and communication plans before an incident.
  • Identification: Detect anomalies or breaches using monitoring tools and alerts.
  • Containment: Short-term containment (stop spread) and long-term containment (prevent recurrence).
  • Eradication: Remove malware, fix vulnerabilities, and verify clean systems.
  • Recovery: Restore systems to normal operation, monitor for signs of weakness.
  • Lessons Learned: Conduct post-incident analysis and update policies.

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Incident Response Team (IRT) coordinates investigation and response.
  • IT staff isolate and recover affected systems.
  • Management communicates with stakeholders and regulators.
  • Employees follow guidance to minimize impact.

Tools & Techniques

  • SIEM (Security Information & Event Management) systems
  • Forensic tools for disk and memory analysis
  • Log monitoring & alerting tools
  • Backup and restore software

Reporting & Compliance

  • Follow legal/regulatory requirements for reporting breaches.
  • Document every action for audit trails.
  • Ensure communication with affected parties is clear and timely.
