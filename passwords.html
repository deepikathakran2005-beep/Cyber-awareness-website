Password Security

Master strong authentication practices and secure password management

Why Strong Passwords Matter

Your password is the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. Weak passwords can be cracked in seconds using automated tools, while strong passwords can take years or even centuries to break.

Compromised passwords are the leading cause of data breaches, affecting both personal and organizational security.

Creating Strong Passwords

Length Matters

Use at least 12-16 characters. Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack.

Mix Character Types

Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters.

Avoid Common Patterns

Don't use sequential characters, common words, or personal information.

‚úó Password123, abc123, YourName2024

Unique for Each Account

Never reuse passwords across different accounts.

‚úì Use a password manager to generate and store unique passwords

Password Best Practices

  • ‚úìUse a reputable password manager to generate and store passwords
  • ‚úìEnable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible
  • ‚úìChange passwords immediately if you suspect a breach
  • ‚úìDon't share passwords via email, text, or messaging apps
  • ‚úìUpdate passwords regularly for critical accounts
  • ‚úìAvoid saving passwords in your browser on shared computers

Pro Tip

Consider using passphrases - memorable sentences with random words, numbers, and symbols. For example: "Coffee!Mountain#9Bicycle@Sunset" is both strong and easier to remember than random characters.
