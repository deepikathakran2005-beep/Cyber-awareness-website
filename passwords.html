Passwords & Authentication - Stay Safe

Learn how to create strong passwords, use password managers, enable 2FA, and prevent attacks like phishing or credential stuffing.

Take Quiz

Why Strong Passwords Are Critical

Passwords are the first line of defense for your accounts. Weak or reused passwords make it easy for attackers to gain unauthorized access. Protecting your credentials reduces the risk of identity theft, financial loss, and data breaches.

Creating Strong Passwords

  • Use **long passphrases**: 12–16+ characters
  • Mix **uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols**
  • Avoid obvious personal info (birthdays, names)
  • Never reuse passwords across accounts
  • Use a **trusted password manager** like Bitwarden or 1Password
  • Enable **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all critical accounts

Common Attacks on Passwords

  • **Brute-force attacks** — systematically guessing passwords
  • **Dictionary attacks** — trying common words and patterns
  • **Credential stuffing** — using leaked passwords from other services
  • **Phishing** — tricking users into revealing credentials
  • **Keylogging and malware** — capturing input directly from the device

Password Managers

Password managers help generate, store, and autofill strong passwords. They reduce the temptation to reuse passwords and can alert you if your accounts are compromised.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an extra layer of security. Even if your password is stolen, attackers cannot log in without the second factor — usually an app code, SMS code, or hardware key.

Recovery & Breach Response

  • Regularly update critical passwords
  • Use recovery options wisely (email, backup codes)
  • Check “Have I Been Pwned” to see if your accounts are compromised
