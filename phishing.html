Phishing Defense

Learn to identify and protect yourself from phishing attacks

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

These attacks typically come through email, text messages, or fake websites designed to look authentic.

How to Spot Phishing Attempts

🔍 Check the Sender

Look closely at the email address. Phishers often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones but contain subtle differences.

⚠️ Urgent Language

Be suspicious of emails creating a sense of urgency, like "Your account will be closed!" or "Immediate action required!"

🔗 Suspicious Links

Hover over links before clicking to see the actual URL. Phishing links often lead to suspicious domains.

📎 Unexpected Attachments

Don't open attachments from unknown senders. They may contain malware or viruses.

Best Practices

  • ✓Verify sender identity before clicking links or providing information
  • ✓Look for spelling and grammar errors - often signs of phishing
  • ✓Access websites directly by typing the URL, not clicking email links
  • ✓Use email filters and spam protection
  • ✓Report suspicious emails to your IT department
  • ✓Never share passwords or sensitive data via email

🚨 If You Suspect Phishing

Don't click any links or download attachments. Delete the email and report it to your organization's security team. If you've already clicked a link, change your passwords immediately and notify IT.
