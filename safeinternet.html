Safe Internet & Social Media Practices

Protect your digital identity, avoid scams and malware, and interact responsibly on social media. Learn practical steps for online safety.

Take Quiz

Introduction

Safe internet usage is vital to protect your personal data, privacy, and reputation. Social media exposes sensitive information that can be misused by cybercriminals. Awareness, vigilance, and best practices are key to staying safe online.

Privacy & Account Security

  • Use unique, strong passwords for every account.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) wherever possible.
  • Regularly check account activity for suspicious logins.
  • Keep devices and software updated to patch vulnerabilities.
  • Limit personal information shared publicly.

Recognizing Threats

  • Phishing messages: links asking for login or personal information.
  • Fake profiles, friend requests, and bots.
  • Malware hidden in attachments or downloads.
  • Scam messages promising money, prizes, or fake jobs.
  • Misleading advertisements and clickbait links.

Safe Social Media Practices

  • Adjust privacy settings to control who sees your posts.
  • Think before posting sensitive information or images.
  • Verify friend requests and account authenticity.
  • Report abusive content and cyberbullying.
  • Regularly audit connected apps and remove unnecessary permissions.

Digital Footprint & Reputation

  • Everything you post online leaves a digital trail.
  • Use pseudonyms for non-essential accounts if needed.
  • Think about long-term consequences of sharing personal content.
  • Use secure and reputable platforms.
