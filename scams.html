Cyber Scams — Recognize, Avoid, and Report

Scammers exploit trust, fear, and urgency to steal your money or information. Learn common scams, warning signs, prevention strategies, and how to recover if targeted.

Take Quiz

Introduction to Scams

Scams are fraudulent schemes aimed at tricking people into giving money, sensitive information, or access to devices. Attackers often manipulate emotions such as fear, greed, or sympathy. Understanding scams is key to online safety.

Common Types of Scams

  • Lottery & Prize Scams: You receive a message claiming you've won a prize and must pay fees to claim it.
  • Romance Scams: Fake relationships on dating apps or social media, aiming to steal money or personal info.
  • Job & Employment Scams: Offers that request personal info or advance payments for fake jobs.
  • Marketplace & E-commerce Scams: Fake listings, overpayments, counterfeit products, or phishing links.
  • Tech Support Scams: Scammers pretend to be support staff asking for remote access or payment to fix non-existent issues.
  • Phishing Emails & SMS: Messages with links to fake websites to capture credentials.
  • Investment & Crypto Scams: Promises of high returns, fake coins, or fraudulent trading platforms.

Psychological Tricks Used by Scammers

  • Urgency: Pressuring victims to act quickly without thinking.
  • Authority: Pretending to be officials, police, banks, or government agencies.
  • Reciprocity: Offering fake gifts or benefits to encourage compliance.
  • Fear: Threatening account suspension, legal action, or loss of assets.
  • Social Proof: Claiming others are already participating or winning.

Red Flags to Identify Scams

  • Unsolicited messages with requests for money or personal information.
  • Poor grammar, spelling errors, or suspicious URLs.
  • Promises that seem “too good to be true”.
  • Pressure to act immediately or bypass standard procedures.
  • Requests for remote access to devices or sharing OTPs/passwords.

Reporting & Recovery

  • Report to your local cybercrime authority or national reporting portal.
  • Contact banks or payment providers if you sent money.
  • Change all affected passwords immediately.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication and monitor accounts for unusual activity.
  • Educate others and share warnings to prevent spread.

Prevention Tips

  • Verify identities, links, and contact details before responding.
  • Use official channels for communication with banks, companies, and government agencies.
  • Install antivirus and anti-malware software and keep it updated.
  • Stay updated about common scams and fraud trends.
  • Be skeptical of unsolicited messages promising rewards or opportunities.

Real-Life Statistics

  • According to reports, online scams cost victims billions globally every year.
  • Romance scams alone have caused losses exceeding $600 million annually worldwide.
  • Tech support scams are responsible for over 2 million complaints each year.
  • Most scam victims report ignoring warning signs due to urgency or emotional manipulation.
